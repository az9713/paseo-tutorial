  ★ Insight ─────────────────────────────────────
  The single idea that dissolves the "magic": Paseo is two halves that people
  merge into one. The daemon is a background worker on your machine that runs
  agents and can read files on your disk. Everything else — phone, web, desktop,
  paseo CLI — is just a remote control.

  So the two things you asked about are completely separate mechanisms that both
  happen to involve the daemon:
  - Scanning the QR connects your phone to the daemon over an encrypted channel.
  It does not read your sessions.
  - Knowing your sessions happens because claude/codex save every chat as files
  on disk (~/.claude/projects/, ~/.codex/sessions/), and the daemon — running on
  that same disk, as you — simply reads the recent ones when you ask. Adopting
  one ("import") is a deliberate click, not automatic surveillance.

  I traced both in the source: pairing in SECURITY.md +
  packages/cli/src/commands/daemon/pair.ts; session discovery in
  packages/server/src/server/agent/agent-manager.ts:615 fanning out to each
  provider's listPersistedAgents(), which for Claude
  (providers/claude/agent.ts:1357) literally opens ~/.claude/projects.
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────

  One thing I deliberately corrected rather than echoed: the phrasing "Paseo is
  aware of all my sessions." The code shows it lists your ~20 most recent
  sessions on demand and filters out empties and already-imported ones — so the
  docs say that, not the overclaim. Same spirit in security.md: I kept the
  honest caveats (in-session replay protection isn't implemented yet; exposing
  the daemon beyond your own machine is your responsibility) instead of sanding
  them off.
